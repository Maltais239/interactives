► WHAT

A maze reading assessment measures how well students understand text they read silently. Every seventh word is replaced with a correct word and two distracters. Students choose the correct word.

► WHY

Capable readers understand syntax and meaning. Students with difficulties often cannot choose words based on semantic accuracy. This assessment identifies these students.

► HOW

Students read a modified passage. The first sentence is intact. For the rest, every seventh word is replaced with parentheses containing the correct word and two distracters. The student circles the correct word.

The length varies from 150 to 400 words. The student has three minutes to complete the task.

150 CORE READING MAZE COMPREHENSION TEST

The student's score is the number of correct words circled in three minutes. Two equivalent passages (A and B) are provided per grade.

► WHAT IT MEANS

Results are reported as correct replacements. This measure is consistent across passages. Low scores may indicate reading difficulties.

If the assessment is used for screening, students scoring significantly lower than expected (about half the table values) may have difficulties. For progress monitoring, typical students show a score increase from beginning to end of year.

History at a glance
1603

King Henri IV granted Pierre Dugua, Sieur de Mons a fur-trade monopoly on the condition that he establish colonies in North America.

1605

After the difficult Saint Croix winter, de Mons and Samuel de Champlain chose the sheltered harbour of Port-Royal and began building a new settlement there.

1606–07

Champlain introduced the Order of Good Cheer to lift morale through shared feasts, food, and ceremony in the Habitation.

1607–1610

When de Mons’ monopoly was revoked, the French left, and Membertou and the Mi’kmaq cared for the site until the settlers returned.

1613

An English expedition from Virginia looted and burned the Habitation while the settlers were away.

1939

The present Habitation is a reconstruction; Parks Canada notes the hand-hewn timbers were crafted during the 1939 rebuilding of the site.
